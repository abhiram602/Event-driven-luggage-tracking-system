Air travel has become increasingly popular worldwide, yet baggage mishandling remains a significant issue causing passenger inconvenience and financial losses for airlines. Traditional barcode-based systems provide limited real-time visibility and often fail to communicate baggage status effectively to passengers. Although RFID technology offers enhanced automation and tracking capabilities, it is expensive and complex. This project proposes a Barcode-Based Airline Passenger Luggage Tracking System as a cost-effective solution. Each piece of luggage is tagged with a unique barcode at check-in, scanned at multiple checkpoints during transit, and tracked via a centralized database. Passengers receive real-time status updates through web or mobile notification, while airline staff can monitor operations via an admin dashboard. This approach improves transparency, reduces mishandling incidents, enhances passenger satisfaction, and bridges the gap between conventional barcode systems and advanced RFID solutions by offering efficient, real-time tracking at lower cost.
